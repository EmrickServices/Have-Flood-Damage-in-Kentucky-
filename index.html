Mold is a type of fungus that thrives on organic materials like wood, paper, drywall and insulation. As it breaks down these materials it releases airborne spores which spread the infection further.

Mold spores that enter an indoor environment quickly reproduce and spread, potentially posing health threats for people allergic to them.

When first discovering mold in your living space, the first step should be removing all porous items like carpets, ceiling tiles, drywall and insulation materials and drying them thoroughly.

Once all affected items have been taken away, use detergent and water to thoroughly clean all hard surfaces. If necessary, scrub any mold off using a stiff brush or cloth; once completed, thoroughly dry all surfaces.

If you need assistance to remove mold, consult with a specialist. They will help remove and prevent further growth of mold.

Mold exposure can have more serious repercussions than just itchy eyes and rashes; it can trigger allergies and worsen asthma in certain people. Furthermore, certain molds produce toxic substances which could make you sick.

Your inspector will carefully examine any signs of flood damage during their property inspection. They will also inspect your ceilings and walls to look for signs of water damage or mold growth, and assess the quality of your insulation.

Inspectors use both pin and electromagnetic moisture meters to measure surface moisture density on your property and can then identify areas in need of restoration and repairs. This process enables them to find hidden spots which need restoration work.

Your inspector may use a thermal camera to detect areas that appear cooler than others within a room – usually an indication of moisture intrusion that’s undetectable by visual inspection alone.

As part of your inspection process, your building inspector will provide an in-depth report with their assessment of damage. One sure way of telling whether or not your property has experienced flood damage is to inspect its interior walls and floors for signs of water damage, such as cracks or soft stains that feel damp to touch. Check the foundation of a house to identify flood damage as another good starting point when inspecting for flooding damage. A damaged foundation can lead to numerous issues, such as rotted piers and beams, leaning walls or disbalance, making it hard for residents to navigate their home freely.

Once you have identified potential foundation issues, it is time to identify any other signs of water damage. Cracked and stained walls running along their height as well as large zig-zag cracks with extensive sections of rotting wood can indicate moisture problems in piers and beams are both telltale signs that something needs attention.
